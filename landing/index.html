How It Works

Image In. Printed Object Out.

Three steps from a photo to a physical object you can hold.

1

Upload a Photo

Take a photo of an existing object — a stand, a holder, a hook — or find a reference image online. Works best for simple mechanical shapes.

2

We Generate a Starting Model

We analyze the image and generate a 3D model that captures the shape. It's a starting point — not a perfect scan — that you can tweak until it's right.

Say "make it taller" or "add a slot for a cable." Once it looks right, export the file and send it straight to your 3D printer.

What It Does

Simple Objects, Printable Fast

Best for everyday objects with clear shapes. Upload a photo, get a model you can print.

Photo to Printable Model

Upload a photo of a simple object. We generate a starting model that captures the shape — something you can refine and print, not a perfect reconstruction.

  • Works best for simple mechanical shapes
  • Supports photos and reference images
  • A model you can tweak — not just a scan

Refine with Plain Language

Describe what to change in plain English. No CAD knowledge needed — just say what you want and the model updates.

"make it 20% taller"
"add a hole for an M4 bolt"
"round all edges by 2mm"
"mirror this on the Y axis"
